## Grid 12 columns

The Orchestra grid is based on the common 12 columns principle.<br />
It is the most divisible number between 1 and 60 (the 6 factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 and 12). It means that there are more possibilities to create different designs than other numbers.

Imagine now that a grid is something that can create rythm on a design. This is the correlation we can make with the music universe.

In music, the grid is related to the **beat** we want to tap when we are listenning a song. This grid is what we called a **time signature**. It can be the same during the whole song or it can change time to time, and it doesn't matter as long as the transition is well done.
In music we can have 12 **eighth notes** in one **bar** on a **12/8 time signature**. The **downbeats** are on the first beat and each 3 beats after, so on the 1, 4, 7 and 10. For example <Anchor href="https://youtu.be/w772GXG5LnE" title="Dazed and Confused by Led Zeppelin on Youtube" isExternal>Dazed and Confused by Led Zeppelin</Anchor> was composed in 12/8. If you pay attention to the main bass line, the notes are played each 3 beats, so on the 1, 4, 7 and 10th beats. Even if the time signature is more complex than usual we can find the rythm of the music with ease.
